Frequently Asked Questions

Different soil types require different crops. You can test your soil to know its pH and nutrient content, then choose crops suited for it, such as tomatoes for loamy soil or corn for sandy soil.

Adding organic matter like compost, using cover crops, and applying natural fertilizers can improve soil fertility over time.

The best time to plant vegetables varies based on climate. Generally, spring and early summer are ideal for many crops. You should consider your local frost dates for planting timing.

Using organic pesticides, introducing beneficial insects, and planting pest-repelling herbs can help keep pests away. Regular crop rotation is also a good practice to minimize pest damage.

Sustainable farming involves using methods that protect the environment, conserve resources, and ensure long-term farm productivity. This includes organic farming, crop rotation, and reducing chemical usage.
